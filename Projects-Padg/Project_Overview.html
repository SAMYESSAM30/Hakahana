Project Overview

PROJECT DESCRIPTION

The project will develop an LPGas Terminal followed by expansion of the current nationwide LPG storage and distribution network to include other SADC countries which includes Botswana, Zambia and Zimbabwe. LPG is the only practical and achievable substitute for firewood and animal dung as a primary fuel source. Increased LPGas consumption improves the environment, improves living conditions, and accelerates economic growth through more efficient use, and enhanced access to energy for households and small businesses.

After the development of the bulk liquid-petroleum-gas storage Hakahana will import bulk gas from Algeria using ships and store it at the LPGas Terminal in Walvis Bay and distrobute it business- to-business and direct to household consumers through an effective supply chain.

The project provides the following deliverables to consumer household beneficiaries:
Environmental
Compared with solid fuels currently used in many households, LPGas reduces ambient air pollution in cities and reduces harmful fumes within households.
Safety
The project will contribute to reducing death and accidents caused by illegal products and inexperienced distributors.
Energy
LPG has a higher calorific value, is more efficient to burn than solid fuels, and provides long-run cost savings to consumers. It is more diverse as it can be used in applications other than just open combustion.
Economic
Households using LPG are more productive, especially women and children, enabling more time for studying and working; family productivity increases. Small businesses benefit from a reliable and uninterrupted fuel supply without the burden of using their own employees or family members to collect firewood. Reduce the dependency on South African exports and thus reduce the price in the region.

IMPACT, OUTCOME, AND BENEFITS

Impacts

Improved access to modern energy sources easing economic growth and well-being, reduced use of traditional solid fuels for energy, private sector investment in SADC’s energy infrastructure.

Outcome

Improved retail supply of affordable, high-quality LPG in a safe and reliable manner with an additional 200,000 households in every country we operate in switching from traditional solid fuels to LPG by 2025.

Benefits

Higher and more dispersed availability of LPG, lower exposure to local pollutants such as suspended particulate matter, a safer and more reliable energy supply system as opposed to the existing gray market system (which is unsafe and unreliable), and alleviation of energy poverty in the country.

The project’s direct benefits include the provision of clean, affordable, reliable energy to households and businesses, and the creation of 300 jobs by end of 2025.

The project significantly benefits women and children, who are often the more vulnerable to the adverse health impacts of solid fuel smoke and suspended particulate matter emissions.

Women and children further benefit by no longer having to spend time collecting firewood, and instead have time to pursue education and other activities that contribute to household productivity and income. Small businesses also receive direct benefits by reducing harmful smoke and particulates in the workplace, increasing worker safety and productivity.

PROJECT SUMMARY

Hakahana LPG is a Namibian LPG Trade entity with an extensive gas distribution network across Namibia consisting of more than 10 depots both industrially, upstream and downstream. The entity occupies a market share of 40% in the local market segment with various opportunities for merging and expansion. Due to the company’s effective business development efforts and reliable customer relations - Hakahana LPG envisaged to scale up operations to the SADC Region with a focus on Zimbabwe, Zambia and Botswana and parts of South Africa.

As such Hakahana LPG set out to establish bulk storage and distribution facilities that would supplement and support these ambitions. Central to this was the competitive bidding and awarding of Hakahana LPG by the Namibian Port Authority

(NamPort) of a concession to develop the first-of-its-kind in Namibia - 20 000 metric ton LPG Storage and Distribution Facility fitted with an offloading pipleine in the Port of Walvis Bay. The entity was allocated the first 120 000 square meters of the availed 820 000 square meters of land and have embarked on peripheral project development activities including:

Engaging regulatory authorities for permitting and relevant permission

Environmental Impact Assessments

Conducting preliminary concept designs

Engaging long-term off-take clients in markets of interest

Hakahana Lacho Power Gas Closed Corporation (Hakahana LPG) is 100% Namibian owned and operated company. The company was founded by Mr Johannes Lacho Abed, a previously disadvantaged Namibian in 2012.
