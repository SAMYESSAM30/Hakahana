Investor Relation

Customers

The main sources of energy in Namibia, Zimbabwe, Zambia and Bostwana are petroleum, biomass, electricity and coal. The final demand for this energy can be split into five main group of consumers which are industry, transport, agriculture, commerce and residential. Transport sector demands the biggest portion of the total energy supply. The final energy demand per sector is shown in the diagram

Residential demand constituted about 28% of the total energy demand, with biomass contributing the bulk towards the demand. Majority of rural households depend on biomass energy. It is in this regard that Hakahana Lacho Power and Gas will mainly target rural and informal settlements in the early stage of LPGas rollout. The coverage of commercial sector will follow, mainly targeting businesses. Thus, the market segmentation for Hakahana Lacho Power and Gas LPGas will be divided between residential, commercial (will include agriculture), and industrial use.

MARKET SEGMENTATION BY COUNTRY

At the moment, Namibia is not self-sufficient in terms of grid electricity and it relies on supply from SA and Zimbabwe. SA and the region are experiencing a surge in demand with SAPP having a shortfall, which resulted in load shedding in Namibia, South Africa, Zambia and Zimbabwe. Regional demand is growing with little generation capacity being created it provides an opportunity for alternative source to be explored. Thus, Hakahana will also target households already on grid- electricity to provide an alternative to electric energy.
