PROJECT DESCRIPTION

The project will develop an LPGas Terminal followed by expansion of the current nationwide LPG storage and distribution network to include other SADC countries which includes Botswana, Zambia and Zimbabwe. LPG is the only practical and achievable substitute for firewood and animal dung as a primary fuel source. Increased LPGas consumption improves the environment, improves living conditions, and accelerates economic growth through more efficient use, and enhanced access to energy for households and small businesses.

After the development of the bulk liquid-petroleum-gas storage Hakahana will import bulk gas from Algeria using ships and store it at the LPGas Terminal in Walvis Bay and distrobute it business- to-business and direct to household consumers through an effective supply chain.

The project provides the following deliverables to consumer household beneficiaries:
Environmental
Compared with solid fuels currently used in many households, LPGas reduces ambient air pollution in cities and reduces harmful fumes within households.
Safety
The project will contribute to reducing death and accidents caused by illegal products and inexperienced distributors.
Energy
LPG has a higher calorific value, is more efficient to burn than solid fuels, and provides long-run cost savings to consumers. It is more diverse as it can be used in applications other than just open combustion.
Economic
Households using LPG are more productive, especially women and children, enabling more time for studying and working; family productivity increases. Small businesses benefit from a reliable and uninterrupted fuel supply without the burden of using their own employees or family members to collect firewood. Reduce the dependency on South African exports and thus reduce the price in the region.
