QHSE

A detailed risk assessment associated with this LPGas project was undertaken and completed by Hakahana Lacho Power and Gas. The mitigation strategies associated with all the risks identified were also concluded to affirm a seamless implementation roll out of the pilot project.

PROCESS HAZARD ASSESSMENT

A process hazard screening will be conducted for the proposed terminal at the start of detailed engineering
and a detailed process hazard assessment (“PHA”) will be conducted when detailed engineering is almost complete.

The detailed PHA will be conducted in compliance with generally accepted industry standards and practices.

PROCESS HAZARD ASSESSMENT

A Fire Safety Plan for the LPGas Terminal will be developed and submitted to the local Fire Chief having jurisdiction over our Terminal in Walvis Bay for review and approval prior to commissioning and start-up of the proposed terminal.

The project will develop an LPGas Terminal followed by expansion of the current nationwide LPG storage and distribution network to include other SADC countries which includes Botswana, Zambia and Zimbabwe. LPG is the only practical and achievable substitute for firewood and animal dung as a primary fuel source. Increased LPGas consumption improves the environment, improves living conditions, and accelerates economic growth through more efficient use, and enhanced access to energy for households and small businesses.

After the development of the bulk liquid-petroleum-gas storage Hakahana will import bulk gas from Algeria using ships and store it at the LPGas Terminal in Walvis Bay and distrobute it business- to-business and direct to household consumers through an effective supply chain.

The Terminal activities will include the receipt of bulk product (LPGas, product storage, and distribution with trucks. Tank Farm Operators will perform preventative maintenance and small repairs.

A BRIEF SUMMARY OF KEY HAKAHANA LACHO LPGAS STORAGE FACILITY POSITIONS FOLLOWS:

Terminal Manager
Operations Manager will be responsible for the general management safe operation of Terminal facility systems and the overall safe movement of LPGas from the trucks to storage facilities then further distribution to retail facilities.
Terminal Supervisor
The terminal Supervisor will supervise all operations staff and will sign off on all Terminal Operators training. The Terminal Supervisor will also oversee the daily execution of LPGas.
Safety, Health and Environment Officer
The health and safety officer’s main responsibility will be to prevent accidents, injuries and work-related illnesses in the workplace. His/ her role will be to create and implement health and safety policies in accordance with the latest legislation and to ensure that these policies are implemented by management and employees
Terminal Operators
Terminal Operators will be responsible for safe execution of routine tasks associated with running the day-to-day operations of the Terminal, which will include daily preventative maintenance activities and small repairs, and daily rounds and inspections. Terminal operators will be trained to move LPGas outside of the Terminal facilities, such as at barges and piping, and will be responsible for issuing and authorizing work permits.
