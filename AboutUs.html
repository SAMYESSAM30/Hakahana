ABOUT
HAKAHANA LPG

Hakahana Lacho Power and Gas is a Namibian company established in 2012 by Johannes Abed. It started as a supplying anchor that establishes and manages terminals coupled with the supplying of liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) across Namibia. Its vision has recently extended to cover Zimbabwe, Zambia and Botswana as well with a 5-years plan to cover all SADC region.
Hakahana Lacho Power and Gas is Commercially registered CC/2015/4970 With Tax Registration: 5880033-01-5 and Social Security: 30111643.

“Establishing Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) depots coupled with supplying LPG gas to Namibia”

Overview /Objectives

Hakahana Lacho Power and Gas has set itself the following key business objectives:

To provide affordable, environmentally friendly and safe energy to
primarily all citizens living in off-grid, pre-grid and ‘grey’ areas in countries
the business operates;

To provide alternative source of energy for thermal usage to grid users as a Demand Side Management measure;

To become the leading and preferred supplier of LPG to all households in countries we operate in; and

To maximize shareholders value through efficient and sustainable
business operations.

BusinessHistory

Hakahana Lacho Power and Gas is a Namibian LPG Trade entity with a 10 years’ experience working in the Namibian territory and with an extensive gas distribution network across Namibia consisting of more than 10 depots both industrially, upstream and downstream. The entity occupies a market share of 40% in the local market segment with various opportunities for merging and expansion .

Due to the company’s effective business development efforts and reliable customer relations - Hakahana LPG envisaged to scale up operations to the SADC Region with a focus on Zimbabwe, Zambia and Botswana and parts of South Africa.

As such Hakahana LPG set out to establish bulk storage and distribution facilities that would supplement and support these ambitions.

Central to this was the competitive bidding and awarding of Hakahana LPG by the Namibian Port Authority (NamPort) of a concession to develop the first-of-its-kind in Namibia - 20 000 metric ton LPG Storage and Distribution Facility fitted with an offloading pipeline in the Port of Walvis Bay.

The entity was allocated the first 120 000 square meters of the availed 820 000 square meters of land and have embarked on peripheral project development activities including:
